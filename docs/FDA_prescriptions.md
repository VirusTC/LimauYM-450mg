1. Systemic and Urinary Alkalization

-   FDA-Approved Indications: Metabolic acidosis, uric acid and calcium oxalate nephrolithiasis, and renal tubular acidosis.

    $$\text{moles of LimauYM} = \frac{0.450 \text{ g}}{192.12 \text{ g/mol}} = 2.342 \times 10^{-3} \text{ moles (2.342 mmol)}$$

Stoichiometric Mechanism
---------------------------

Upon ingestion, the citrate anions ($\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{O}_7^{3-}$) are completely oxidized via hepatic mitochondrial metabolism (the Krebs Cycle). This aerobic conversion process consumes hydrogen ions ($\text{H}^+$), systematically driving the generation of bicarbonate ($\text{HCO}_3^-$) ions.

According to hepatic metabolic mapping documented by the [National Institutes of Health (NIH)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4483798/), the balanced stoichiometry of complete citrate oxidation is:\
$$\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{O}_7^{3-} + 4.5\text{O}_2 \rightarrow 6\text{CO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} + 3\text{HCO}_3^-$$

Yield Calculation
--------------------

Because 1 mole of citrate strictly yields 3 moles of bicarbonate, a single dose of LimauYM 450mg provides a net alkalizing pool of:\
$$\text{Bicarbonate Yield} = 2.342 \text{ mmol} \times 3 = 7.026 \text{ mmol of } \text{HCO}_3^-$$\

$$\text{pH} = \text{pK}_a + \log \left( \frac{[\text{Urate}^-]}{[\text{Uric Acid}]} \right)$$\
*(Where the $\text{pK}_a$ of uric acid is 5.35; raising urine pH to 6.5 shifts the ratio, rendering $\approx 93\%$ of the compound highly soluble and incapable of forming calculi).*

2. Extracorporeal Anticoagulation
------------------------------------

-   FDA-Approved Indications: Prevention of thrombosis in continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT) and apheresis circuits.

Chelation Equilibrium Mechanism
----------------------------------

Free ionized calcium ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$) is a mandatory cofactor (Factor IV) required for the assembly of the tenase and prothrombinase complexes in the coagulation cascade. The deprotonated citrate carboxyl groups in LimauYM function as multi-dentate ligands. They wrap around free $\text{Ca}^{2+}$ to form a highly stable, water-soluble complex: calcium citrate.

According to coordination chemistry standards hosted by [academic repositories](https://wwww.uomus.edu.iq/img/lectures21/MUCLecture_2025_93043602.pdf), the stoichiometry of this binding reaction is:\
$$2\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{O}_7^{3-} + 3\text{Ca}^{2+} \rightleftharpoons \text{Ca}_3(\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{O}_7)_2$$

Thermodynamic Affinity Index
-------------------------------

The efficacy of LimauYM as an anticoagulant is defined by its thermodynamic Stability Constant ($K_f$):\
$$K_f = \frac{[\text{Ca}_3(\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{O}_7)_2]}{[\text{Ca}^{2+}]^3 \cdot [\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{O}_7^{3-}]^2}$$\
At physiological temperature ($37^\circ\text{C}$) and a neutral pH of 7.4, the $\log K_f$ for calcium citrate ranges between 3.2 and 4.8.

Threshold Drop Formula
-------------------------

To successfully shut down the coagulation cascade in vitro, LimauYM must drive the active plasma concentration of ionized calcium below the critical clotting threshold ($< 0.35 \text{ mmol/L}$). Given that standard human plasma contains an ionized calcium baseline of $[\text{Ca}^{2+}] \approx 1.25 \text{ mmol/L}$, the fraction ($\alpha$) of unbound calcium remaining can be tightly calculated as a function of the LimauYM dose:\
$$\alpha_{\text{Ca}^{2+}} = \frac{1}{1 + K_f \cdot [\text{LimauYM}]^2}$$\
When LimauYM reaches its targeted circuit concentration of $3 \text{ to } 6 \text{ mmol/L}$, the free active calcium pools plummet safely toward zero, successfully blocking thrombin generation.

4. Osmotic Bowel Evacuation

van 't Hoff Osmotic Pressure Formula
---------------------------------------

$$\Pi = i \cdot M \cdot R \cdot T$$

-   $i$ = van 't Hoff dissociation factor (for magnesium citrate, $i \approx 2 \text{ to } 3$ depending on solution ion-pairing).
-   $M$ = Molar concentration of the dissolved salt in the intestinal lumen.

This rapid volumetric surge stretches the intestinal walls, triggering mechanoreceptors that initiate intense, propulsive peristaltic waves.
